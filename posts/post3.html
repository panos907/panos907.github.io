Routine, stress and coping

Edited: 13/03/2025

I have often been told i need to avoid routine, and need to avoid getting stressed as much as i do. The former is relatively easy (or not) and the latter is rather hard (or perhaps not). Curious how many things aren't what we perceived at first glance, isn't it? Allow me to elaborate...

As far as routine is concerned, i do have one, like everybody does. I have a job, i have Aikido training i attend, i have my weekly Vampire The Masquarade game i play. Yet, apart from these, everything else is pretty much in the air, up for grabs by any friend (or otherwise) that might request my attention kindly. But of course, there is also the "everyday" routine, what most people fail to consider because it's been so deeply ingrained in their brains it is now automatic. Cooking, brushing teeth, showering, changing clothes, commuting to work, all these are also part of it, taking up a (significant sometimes) chunk of time on their own right. Not to mention, that a lot of us (myself included) might even struggle with some parts of that at times, for different reasons. Yet another thing many people are painfully oblivious towards. And when it happens to them, they panic as if their whole world has been turned upside down. Not knowing that a shifted routine can be much more easily restored than a damaged body or a scarred soul.

Stress, on the other hand. Is a tricky one. Many people that have it don't notice, and many people that notice it can't/don't know how to deal with it (what we call coping). To go to a bit of a tangent, the past few weeks, i have been noticing that i am MUCH more stressed than i initially thought. But, the various coping mechanisms i have developed and continue to develop (mainly deep breathing exercises) allow me to cope with it all while maintaining my sanity at an acceptable level and still managing to thrive despite it all.

...Or so i thought. Turns out, a sedentary lifestyle can quickly catch up to you in unexpected ways, and piling too much stuff on your plate at once does little to help the issue. And while i have succesfully managed to identify some stressors and remove/reduce them (like my weekly VTM game that i didn't actually enjoy as much as i thought i would), others are not so easily dealt with. There's work, there's the freelance gigs after work, there's "research"/reading stuff about various hobbies or potential new ones (which, don't get me wrong, is pretty fun, but can get take it's toll on you when it's 3AM and you re reading about different kinds of microphones that you re not going to be using in the near future, as an example). A youtuber i used to watch, named Adam Duff, gave me a wake-up call on this in one of his videos, where he mentioned a pretty similar quote: "i realized i didnt HAVE to stay up so late reading about camera lights". Better late than ever, i guess...

Guess i decided to break the routine of uploading a new post by editing an old one instead. Hopefully the next time i write something on here, it will be from a place of a more clear head, a more relaxed body and less-stressed muscles.

Till then, you take care of yourself as well!
